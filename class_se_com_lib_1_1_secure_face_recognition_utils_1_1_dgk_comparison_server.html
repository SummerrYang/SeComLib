Implementation of Protocol 4.10, as described in Martin Franz' Master Thesis from 2008 ( \( a = [\hat{d}] \), \( b = \hat{r} \)

Fetch \( \hat{d} \) from the client

Compute the \( e \) vector

If \( \hat{r}_{l - 1} = s \)

Warning
Protocol 4.10 from Martin Franz' Master Thesis DOES NOT take into account the two different cases when \( \hat{r}_{l - 1} = 0 \) and \( \hat{r}_{l - 1} = 1 \) so the protocol does not work properly for values that differ starting with the MSB!

\( [e_{l - 1}] = [0] \) if \( (d_{l - 1} < r_{l - 1}) \land s = 1 \) or if \( (d_{l - 1} > r_{l - 1}) \land s = 0 \)

\( [e_{l - 1}] = [\hat{d}_{l - 1} - 1]^{R_{l - 1}}) = (([\hat{d}_{l - 1}] [1]^{-1})^{R_{l - 1}}) \)

\( [e_{l - 1}] = ([\hat{d}_{l - 1}]^{R_{l - 1}}) \)

\( [e_{l - 1}] = [e_{l - 1}]_{re-rand} \)

\( [e_{l - 1}] = [R_{l-1}] \)

\( [\sigma] = xor([\hat{d}_{l - 1}], \hat{r}_{l - 1}) \)

If \( b_i = s \)

\( [c] = [\hat{d}_{i} + \sigma] = [\hat{d}_{i}] [\sigma] \)

\( [c] = [c - 1 + \sigma] = [c] [-1] [\sigma] \)

\( [e_i] = ([c]^{R_i})_{re-rand}\)

\( [e_i] = [R_i] \)

\( [\sigma] = [\sigma] xor([\hat{d}_{i}], \hat{r}_{i}) \)

To avoid the case \( \hat{d} = \hat{r} \), we compare \( 2 \hat{d} + 1 \) and \( 2 \hat{r} \) instead (append differing bits that don't change the final result) Due to this, \( 2 \hat{d} + 1 \) and \( 2 \hat{r} \) will have (at most) \( l + 1 \) bits

In order to achieve this, we know the values of the new LSB of \( \hat{d} \) and \( \hat{r} \): \( LSB(\hat{d}) = 1 \) and \( LSB(\hat{r}) = 0 \)

\( [e_{-1}] = [R_{-1}] \)

\( [e_{-1}] = ([\sigma]^{R_{-1}})_{re-rand}\)

Apply a random permutation to vector \( e \)

\( [\lambda] = [-2^l] [\lambda]^{-1} \)
